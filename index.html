History Of Cox's Bazar

Cox's Bazar is named after Captain Hiram Cox, an officer of the East India Company, who was assigned with the charges of the current day Cox's Bazar and its adjacent areas. The town of Cox's Bazar was established in 1799 as a market town to honour Captain Cox. The sea beach is the main attraction of the Cox's Bazar town with an unbroken 155 Km length.
